## Complete Example: ScripRight

> Hi, my name is Barbara and I'm with Dave and we are ScripRight. Family practitioners can struggle to safely and effectively prescribe medications to patients with complicated health issues, leading to accidental side effects and toxicities that cost the US healthcare industry $3 billion annually in avoidable medical interventions. At Scripright we are solving this problem by providing family doctors with a decision making software platform that provides the most up-to-date drug information in real time so doctors can prescribe the right drugs at the right dose and at the right time.

## PRO TIPS

### 1. Play around with the order of elements 1-3
- Try introducing your venture name first, OR
- Start with the problem first
- Experiment to find what flows most naturally

### 2. Practice, practice, practice
- Don't read your intro
- If you're passionate about solving the problem, this will come naturally
- Memorize the flow, not word-for-word

### 3. Keep it under 30 seconds
- Use short, clear statements
- Devoid of technical jargon
- Every word should count
- Practice with a timer

---

## Key Principles for Your Elevator Pitch

### ✅ DO:
- Keep sentences simple and short
- Focus on the problem and its impact
- Highlight what makes your solution better
- Show the value/benefit to customers
- Include quantifiable impact when possible (e.g., "$3 billion annually")
- Speak conversationally and naturally

### ❌ DON'T:
- Use technical jargon or industry buzzwords
- Include too many details
- Describe technical features (focus on benefits)
- Go over 30 seconds
- Read from a script during delivery

---

## Crafting Tips for Each Section

### Problem Statement (Section 4)
- Who has the problem?
- What is the consequence/impact?
- Include numbers if possible (cost, time lost, scale)
- Make it relatable and compelling

### Solution Description (Section 5)
- Focus on WHAT you do, not HOW it works technically
- Be specific about the delivery method (platform, service, product)
- Keep it simple enough for anyone to understand

### Competitive Advantage (Section 6)
- What makes you different/better?
- Focus on customer benefits, not technical features
- What can you do that others can't?
- Why would customers choose you?

## Practice Checklist

Before you finalize your elevator pitch, check:

- [ ] Can be delivered in under 30 seconds
- [ ] No technical jargon
- [ ] Problem is clear and compelling
- [ ] Solution is easy to understand
- [ ] Competitive advantage is obvious
- [ ] Flows naturally when spoken out loud
- [ ] Passionate and authentic delivery
- [ ] Tested with someone outside your industry
