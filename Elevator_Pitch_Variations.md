## Delivery Best Practices

### Tone:
- **Confident** (not arrogant)
- **Passionate** (not overly emotional)
- **Clear** (not jargony)

### Pacing:
- **Slow down** for key numbers (40-50 pairs, 45 days, $100K)
- **Speed up slightly** when listing benefits/features
- **Pause** before transitions ("Here's what makes us different...")

### Energy:
- **Start strong** ‚Äî grab attention immediately
- **Build** through problem and solution
- **Peak** at unfair advantage
- **Close powerfully** ‚Äî memorable tagline

### Body Language:
- **Open posture** ‚Äî no crossed arms
- **Hand gestures** ‚Äî emphasize key points
- **Eye contact** ‚Äî scan the room, don't fixate
- **Smile** when appropriate (passion, vision sections)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Too much jargon:
- Avoid: "Sneaca leverages vertical integration..."
- Better: "My family owns the factory, so we control the whole process"

### ‚ùå Burying the lead:
- Avoid: Starting with "The footwear industry is $365 billion globally..."
- Better: Start with the problem or the unfair advantage

### ‚ùå Rushing through numbers:
- Avoid: "40to50pairsin45daysfor2500dollars"
- Better: "Forty to fifty pairs... delivered in forty-five days... starting at twenty-five hundred dollars"

### ‚ùå Underselling the advantage:
- Avoid: "We have good supplier relationships"
- Better: "My family **owns** the factory ‚Äî a 5-10 year moat"

### ‚ùå Forgetting the human element:
- Avoid: Purely rational/business case
- Better: "We're giving communities the power to wear their identity"
