## Handling Common Objections

### Q: "Can your factory handle the demand?"
**A:** "Great question. The factory produces 50,000+ pairs per month. Our Year 1 target is ~10,000 pairs total ‚Äî less than 1% of capacity. Scalability is not a constraint."

### Q: "Why can't competitors just find their own factory?"
**A:** "Building trusted factory relationships takes 5-7 years. Most factories won't prioritize 50-200 pair runs because it's operationally complex. Our family connection ensures priority access and specialized small-batch SOPs that take years to develop."

### Q: "Isn't FIFA 2026 a short-term opportunity?"
**A:** "FIFA is our **beachhead**, not our business. It's a high-visibility launch moment that proves our quality and execution. Our core business is custom footwear for communities, corporations, and hospitality ‚Äî FIFA just gives us concentrated demand to establish credibility."

### Q: "What about quality control?"
**A:** "We target 98.5%+ First Pass Yield with pre-production sample approval, QA checkpoints at every stage, and family reputation ensuring attention to detail. Our factory's existing quality standards are world-class."

### Q: "How will you acquire customers?"
**A:** "Three channels: (1) Partnerships with 10 promo agencies and FIFA venues, (2) Outbound sales with 2 BDRs targeting 200 accounts, (3) Content marketing showcasing use cases and AI mockups. FIFA community capsules seed our brand with cultural organizations."

### Q: "What if production costs increase?"
**A:** "We've built margin protection into our pricing tiers with cost ceilings. For example, at the $55 tier, our landed cost ceiling is $27.50 to maintain 50% margin. We also have setup fees for high-volume tiers to protect margin structure."

### Q: "Who are your direct competitors?"
**A:** "There are no direct competitors serving the 40-500 pair segment. Traditional manufacturers require 1,000+ pairs. Promotional distributors offer generic stock items with logo embroidery, not true custom manufacturing. We're filling a white space."

## Body Language & Delivery Tips

### Energy Levels:
- **High energy:** Slides 3 (60s intro), 8 (Why Now), 12 (Ask)
- **Confident & clear:** Slides 7 (Solution), 10 (Competitive), 11 (Business Model)
- **Empathetic:** Slides 4 (Problem), 5 (Customer)

### Hand Gestures:
- **Problem (Slide 4):** Show frustration, closed fist (representing barriers)
- **Solution (Slide 7):** Open hands, flowing motion (representing ease)
- **Unfair Advantage (Slide 2):** Point to yourself (representing ownership)

### Vocal Variety:
- **Slow down** for critical numbers (MOQs, margins, revenue targets)
- **Speed up** slightly when listing benefits/features
- **Pause** before key transitions ("Here's what makes us different...")

### Eye Contact:
- Scan the room during storytelling sections (Slides 3, 4, 8)
- Direct eye contact when stating numbers (Slides 9, 11, 12)
- Look confident during competitive slide (Slide 10)
