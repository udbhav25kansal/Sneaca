## Presentation Requirements

### Template Instructions:
1. **Download** the official presentation template
2. **Make a copy** for your team
3. **Delete** all guidance text before submitting
4. **Follow** the specific slide structure (do not omit any subject matter)
5. **Can customize:** Slide titles and slide order
6. **Cannot customize:** Must cover all required topics

### Presentation Guidelines:
- **Duration:** 7-10 minutes (you will be stopped at time limit)
- **Focus:** Compelling and logical story about your venture opportunity
- **Goal:** Show you understand your venture's position in your industry ecosystem
- **Style:** Slides are visual aids, not scripts - don't read from them
- **Language:** Reduce jargon wherever possible

## Interview Assessment Criteria

The panel will be evaluating:

### 1. **Team**
- Skills and background
- UBC connections
- Commitment levels (full-time vs part-time)
- Realistic time allocation

### 2. **Venture Opportunity**
- Problem clarity and impact
- Market size and potential
- Solution effectiveness
- Competitive differentiation

### 3. **Underlying Technology**
- Technology readiness level
- Product/service maturity
- Intellectual property status
- Development timeline

### 4. **Program Readiness**
- Why Venture Founder program?
- What you want to achieve
- Areas where you need help
- Next 12-month goals (4 T's: Team, Tech, Traction, Treasury)

---

## Key Success Factors

### To Make a Strong Impression:

1. **Tell a Compelling Story**
   - Logical flow from problem to solution
   - Show passion for solving the problem
   - Make it relatable and memorable

2. **Show Market Understanding**
   - Know your industry ecosystem
   - Understand competitive landscape
   - Demonstrate customer knowledge

3. **Be Clear and Concise**
   - Simple language, no jargon
   - Clear value proposition
   - Specific, measurable goals

4. **Demonstrate Commitment**
   - Realistic time commitments
   - Clear next steps
   - Honest assessment of needs

5. **Engage Professionally**
   - Don't read from slides
   - Handle Q&A thoughtfully
   - Show you've done your research
